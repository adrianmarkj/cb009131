DEHIWALA ZOO

The Zoo

The Dehiwala National Zoo is one of Asia's oldest zoos, with a spectacular array of native and indigenous fauna.

Dehiwala Zoo currently houses 72 species of mammals, 65 species of birds, 31 species of reptiles, 89 species of fish, three families of amphibians, and 30 species of butterflies, among other animals. Because of breeding and free-living bird colonies, the total number of animals ranges between 2500 and 3000.

With a new website and inclusion in several significant redevelopment projects, the Zoo is keeping up with the rapidly changing world, with the goal of improving the Zoo's overall reputation while ensuring optimum services for animals and visitors.

The Animals

The Dehiwala Zoo boasts a multitude of animals to be seen in all their glory; some local species and even some foreign species.

The Zoo contains the largest variety of animals from amongst all zoological centres in the country.

The Top 5 Most Popular Animals in the Zoo Are:

  1. the Sri Lankan Elephant
  2. the Sri Lankan Leopard
  3. Peacocks
  4. the Sri Lankan Junglefowl
  5. the Grey Hornbill

The Plants

Sri Lanka's virgin forests are abundant in flora and fauna, boasting a broad spectrum of species. This diverse collection of plants has its applications ranging from medicinal, natural dyes and crops.

With 32.2% of the country's total land area being forest cover, it is no doubt that the locals pride themselves on intricate displays of gardening uses trees, shrubs and flowering plants of all kinds.

The Zoo Contains Plants from the Families:

  • Acanthaceae
  • Amaranthaceae
  • Caryophyllaceae
  • Ericaceae
